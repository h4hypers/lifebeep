About LifeBeep

Your Personal Sound Guardian

Real-Time Sound Detection

Uses an advanced microphone module to capture surrounding sounds continuously, ensuring no critical alert is missed.

IC 741 Analog Band-Pass Filter

Custom-built analog op-amp circuit filters specific frequency ranges (1.8-2.3V threshold) to detect loud noise events with precision.

ESP32 Processing Power

The ESP32 microcontroller performs advanced frequency analysis and decision-making to classify sound types in real-time.

Multi-Modal Alerts

Vibration motor, LED flash, OLED display, and optional buzzer work together to ensure you never miss an important sound.

24/7 Wearable Design

Designed as a comfortable watch-style wearable device that provides continuous protection day and night.

Instant Safety Response

From sound detection to alert notification in milliseconds, ensuring rapid response to emergency situations.

Target Audience

Built for Fully and Partially Hearing-Impaired Users

Born with complete hearing loss - LifeBeep provides tactile and visual alerts without requiring any hearing ability.

12% Partial Congenital Impairment

30% Other Impairments

Age-related, accident-induced, or disease-related hearing loss - LifeBeep offers independence and safety.

Primary Focus: LifeBeep targets the 20% of the population with complete or partial hearing impairment, offering an affordable, wearable solution for enhanced safety and independence.

System Architecture

How LifeBeep Works: From Sound to Alert

Block Diagram

Please add your block diagram image to static/images/block-diagram.png
1

Surrounding Sound Source

Environmental sounds (car horn, fire alarm, baby crying, etc.) are emitted in the user's vicinity.

2

Microphone Module

High-sensitivity microphone captures acoustic signals and converts them to electrical signals.

3

IC 741 Analog Band-Pass Filter

Custom op-amp circuit filters signals between 1.8-2.3V threshold, isolating loud noise events.

4

ESP32 Frequency Analysis

Microcontroller processes filtered signals, performs frequency analysis, and classifies sound types.

5

Decision Unit

Intelligent logic determines threat level and triggers appropriate alert mechanisms.

6

OLED Display

Visual feedback shows sound type, alert level, and system status with animations.

7

Vibration Motor

Tactile alert through wrist vibration - works 24/7, even during sleep.

8

LED Indicator

Visual safety signal with color-coded alerts (red = danger, yellow = warning, green = safe).

9

User (Hearing-Impaired Person)

Receives multi-modal alerts and can respond appropriately to environmental sounds.
